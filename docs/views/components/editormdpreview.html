# Agra-Black The paving slabs in front of the grand estate home, were a little uneven. Workers laid it during the mansions' construction back in 1740, in what was known in the trade as a basket weave pattern. It was pleasant to look at, but not remarkable. What *was* remarkable is that it meant *'Insert Rug Here'* in Caspiodatisk, the language spoken by the two driving the van. The very van which jolted under heavy braking and now halted on those uneven slabs. The van had a Damask pattern woven on the rear sides and on the back doors. The name 'Agra-Black' in a calligraphy script, with block letters; *Silk Rug Company* ran on its side, a little larger than one would expect a signage professional with a keen eye would recommend. The doors opened. A man, smaller than average, hopped out into the light rain. He wore a New York printed cap and a black rain poncho. There was something odd about him. His nose, large and slightly hooked, dominated his face. His ears looked like it was clipped on and if you looked closely, indeed it was. A metal band ran around the back of his head like a spring, clamping two plastic ears in place. He let out what sounded like a medieval jingle, with a throaty and nasal overtone whistle. He wore a leg brace, the kind where one leg is slightly shorter, and he limped to the back of the van, opening the double doors. The second man appeared from the other side of the van. He was the same height as the first, had the same plastic ears and if you knew anything about the Caspiodates, you would know that they looked much the same and only they could tell each other apart. The second man whistled a short and stunted tune, prompting the first man to climb into the van before both emerging, carrying a silk rug. The rug, despite being rolled up, was clearly splendid, with dark maroons and reds and the trademarked fine gold fringe. Agra-Black made the world's finest silk rugs, everyone knew it. It was revered by the master makers in Persia and beyond, quality rugs that even the great Maya Devar Al-Aziz could not explain. The master craftsmen of these wondrous rugs, remained mysterious, despite tabloids, journalists, even social media stars, working hard to uncover their secrets. Scores of journalists tried following the delivery vans, only to be evaded and foiled every time. Agra-Black operated in secrecy, their carpets and methods unpatented, for fear of others knowing how to make them. Of course, the real scandal was that earthlings had no idea about who the Caspiodates were, or why they were here selling silk rugs. The two men carried the silk rug and clanged the door knocker on the tall and heavy wooden carved doors. One side creaked open, revealing a footman in his black trousers, and mustard waistcoat. He was a lot taller than the Caspiodates. "Gentleman," lowering his head, "Kindly use the back entrance for deliveries." The footman stepped back and closed the door. Thud. The Caspiodates carrying the rug, present a particular challenge when trying to differentiate. Even their names present a challenge, as there is no proper way to write whistles, with any precision. The first Caspiodate will be known as York, after the cap he wears. The other Caspiodate's name sounds similar to "whww-wh-hiiii?", the question mark, an attempt at adding the raised ending. We will also have to translate their communication whistles into English, else it would be rather confusing. Whiiii says, "Right, Folla me." You will notice that he also has a strong Equivian accent, as he was brought up in foster care. Whiiii and York, walked around the entire house, as unfortunately, they went to the right, and the side door was only fifty feet away if they went to the left. Their journey around the entirety of the house, unfortunately led them to some pesky children, playing on the grass. Whiiii and York traipsed carefully, hoping to go unnoticed, but children always noticed. Children knew they were odd. "Hey, Jane," called out one boy, "Look at the aliens." The girl laughed and pointed. The nanny, told them to not be so rude and apologised pulling the boy and the girl further back onto the grass. York gave a little wave and an awkward nod. Whiiii scowled, picking up speed which came as a surprise to York, who almost fell. The footman greeted them at the back entrance, "This way." They followed the footman through a kitchen, a long hallway and then up some tight stairs. They walked along what was a servants' passage and exited into a grand room, out of a bookshelf's secret door. "Over here, gentleman." The footman entered the room and waved his arm to gesture they enter, which they did, clumsily. Whiiii jabbed a whistle and they set the rug down, in the open cleared area, rolling it out. The room had a gold chandelier, oil paintings of horses, with amazing wooden floors and a grand dining table with 18 chairs, moved to the side. As the rug settled into place, immediately four house staff, picked up the large dining table and set it on the rug, another ensuring it was exactly central to the chandelier. One of the rug's corners got kicked and turned over by one of the staff, to which York flung himself to the corner, setting it straight. "Thank you, Gentleman," said the footman, "If you would escort me now to the kitchen, I will ensure a biscuit and tea if you so desire." The footman turned and left through the bookcase, but York and Whiiii seemed reluctant to leave the rug. "This way gentleman," called the footman. Whiiii said, "Whee-twee-wii-woo-?-whoo?" York replied and then went on his hands and knees, extracting a fringe comb from his pocket and proceeded to straighten the fringe around the carpet. Whiiii walked around inspecting the rug and on noticing a slight crossing of the fringe on the left side, also collapsed to hands and knees, combing it out. The footman, returned and now standing in the bookshelf's doorway, coughed, "Gentleman, if you would kindly follow me." Whiiii jumped up awkwardly, extracted a card from his inside jacket pocket and handed it to the footman. Important was written in bold on the one side and turning it over, it read. *Do not clean.* *Maintain the fringe with a comb, always extended, never bent.* The footman read the instruction, to which Whiiii held out the fringe comb, beckoning him to take it. He mimed the combing of the fringe and urged the footman once more. "Yes, yes," laughed the footman, "We have a dozen housekeepers here who are well versed in this sort of thing." Whiiii turned to look at the staff, and limped over. He removed a dozen or so cards from his pockets, handing one to each member of staff. He mimed the combing of the fringe, again and again. Everyone was amused and nodded to the pantomime. The footman commanded, "Gentleman, I must insist." They followed the footman down the passage and the stairs. Whiiii continued to hand out a card to a member of staff they passed in the passage. They mimed the combing of the fringe as they walked past two other members of bemused staff. "Yes," said footman, "I see you are extremely passionate about your rugs." They responded with a quirky smile and followed him to the kitchen, where one of the kitchen staff had two cups of tea and biscuits on a plate. "I shall leave you. Thank you gentleman." The footman was gone. Whiiii and York drank the tea in two large gulps, devoured a biscuit and then left the house, still handing out cards. They made it past the distasteful children and into their van. They took off at great speed, as with all things Caspiodate, a little exaggerated. Brakes were not pressed, but slammed, accelerator not depressed gently, but treated as an on-off switch. The gates closed behind them and then screeched off down the road. *** The paranoid white van drove down East India Dock road, made a quick turn onto the A1026. They took the turn and accelerated on the A1261, only to be stopped dead by traffic, leaving them in a vulnerable position of being tracked. When the traffic gave way, they were turning right back onto the dock road, and back to the roundabout. They tried again. They circled the roundabout a few times more and then successfully, disappeared. As it happens, a reporter from the local newspaper had been following them, but lost them at the roundabout. *** The white van entered through triple sized roller doors into a vacuous building and parked, alongside four other vans of the same kind. Another van, followed them in and parked alongside. Whiiii and York, were almost indistinguishable from a dozen or so other Caspiodates getting out of the vehicles and who were converging to rows of unfolded seats, arranged in front of a stage. Almost indistinguishable if it wasn't for them both being a metre taller than the others. More vans came in until there were thirty eight of them seated. They all stood up and clapped as another Caspiodate, wearing a red fascinator on the side of his head took the stage. He raised his arms and asked them to settle down. "wheee-whoo-tree-riii-aiii," he whistled. Everyone applauded. The Caspiodate removed his jacket and hung it over the chair. He reached into a box and pulled something out, which he obscured in his palms. "Our instructions are now," he said, stirring anticipation, "Red." "Much, much better," said a voice. "Hooray," said another. York turned to Whiiii with a large grin, "Much better. It can't miss." A tear came to the right eye of Whiiii, who wiped it away. Only his right eye shed a tear, as Caspiodates had directional tears. So embarrassed by crying, their species had evolved to only tear up in whichever eye faced away from other people. "A red back, represents danger. We feel they will respond better to this colour." "But also," he continued, "The instructions are now," he turned the card over, "in bold." This took the crowd over the edge with a standing ovation, and great cheering. It was so chaotic that it took a while before they all settled back into their chairs. The event concluded and they all went off in different directions, some packed rugs into their vans, others were laughing and chatting, others doing paperwork. York and Whiiii still feeling good about the meeting, gave each other a hug and then wandered off to an offshoot meeting. The Caspiodate who was on stage, was now talking to a few others around a whiteboard. He was clearly in charge, and he pointed to a chart showing deliveries over the last month. Whiiii and York added themselves to the conversation. "And we can possibly open the gateway on the 4th October," one Caspiodate said. "Even earlier, if we can get these final sixty deliveries out," said another. Whiiii added, "We have five deliveries tomorrow." "Whiiii, nice of you to join us," said the boss, "Our star delivery team. Hello York, join us." They all shuffled properly making a gap in their circular huddle. "These guys have delivered the most rugs in the shortest time," the boss said, "And the most promotions." "We only want a successful invasion," said Whiiii. York nodded, "All we do is for that glorious day." "The fringe problem is still a problem and I am pleased to see the new cards," said Whiiii. "I designed it," said one of the gathered. Whiiii congratulated him with a pat on the back and said, "We have to get the message through. It is difficult to convince humans to maintain the fringe." "So untidy." "Such neglect." "It's as if they just don't care." Whiiii assured them, "This new card, is the way to go." He smiled again at the designer. They all whistled and whistled, and it sounded like a Celtic celebration of the harvest. Whiiii looked at the boss and they both noticed. They stood up straight, the boss on tip toes, prompting Whiiii to also stretch up. You see, every time a rug was complimented, the Caspiodates who delivered it, were rewarded with a promotional height increase. The Caspiodates, were of course not the only race in the galaxy to be governed purely by heightocracy, starting out very tiny. "Fantastic," said one of the others. "Now, back to business." Whiiii, the new boss took a step forward assuming his position in the circle. "Remember, we are to flick the switch, activating all the portals at 7pm that evening. We think it will be the 4th of next month." "But it could be sooner," said Dave. "Yes. Now we will flick the activation switch, and send the signal, to which the invasion force from Caspionate will enter Earth through our portals." "I can't wait," giggled another.
